## Bitget Copy Trading: A Hidden Gem

Copy trading is arguably Bitget's most differentiated product, and it's one of the main reasons the platform attracts traders who wouldn't otherwise switch from Binance or Bybit.

### How It Works

Copy trading allows you to automatically mirror the trades of experienced traders in real time. When a trader you follow opens a position, the same trade opens proportionally in your account. When they close, yours closes too.

You set:
- **Copy amount** — how much capital to allocate
- **Maximum position size** — a per-trade risk cap
- **Stop-loss threshold** — automatically stop copying if your drawdown exceeds a set amount

### Finding Traders to Follow

Bitget's copy trading leaderboard shows metrics for each eligible trader:
- Win rate (% of trades that were profitable)
- Total profit (USD) over selectable timeframes
- Maximum drawdown (how much loss before recovery)
- Number of followers
- Average holding time

You can filter by futures or spot copy traders, by ROI, by trade frequency, or by asset focus. Traders with 6–18 month track records tend to be more reliable than those with 30-day performance spikes.

### What It Costs

Copy trading itself doesn't charge a separate platform fee. However, traders on the leaderboard can set a **profit share rate** — typically 5–10% of your profits generated through their trades. You only pay this if you're profitable. The normal trading fees still apply to each copied trade.

### Who It's For

Copy trading is genuinely useful if:
- You understand markets but don't have time to actively trade
- You want exposure to crypto volatility without doing technical analysis yourself
- You're learning and want to observe how skilled traders manage positions

It's not a guaranteed income stream, and past performance doesn't guarantee future results. Treat it as a tool, not a passive income machine.
